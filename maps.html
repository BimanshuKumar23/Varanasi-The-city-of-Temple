Varanasi Ghats

Ghats in Varanasi refer to a series of steps leading down to the riverbank of the Ganges. The city has around 80 ghats, each with its own name and associated legends and rituals. The ghats are used for various purposes, including religious ceremonies, bathing, cremation, and spiritual practices. Some of the prominent ghats in Varanasi include Dashashwamedh Ghat (the main and most vibrant ghat), Manikarnika Ghat (the principal cremation ghat), Assi Ghat, Harishchandra Ghat, and Panchganga Ghat, among others. These ghats are not only important religious sites but also serve as popular tourist attractions, offering a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage of the city. The ghats of Varanasi are known for their distinctive architecture, bustling activity, and the mesmerizing sight of devotees performing rituals and ceremonies. Taking a boat ride along the river at dawn or dusk, witnessing the aarti (a religious ritual involving fire) at Dashashwamedh Ghat, or simply observing the life and rituals along the ghats can be a memorable and spiritual experience for visitors
